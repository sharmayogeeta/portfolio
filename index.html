Decoding Dominance: A Discourse Analysis of Power Structures in Contemporary Media Narratives

ESP International Journal of Science, Humanities and Management Studies
Abstract: In an era where media narratives significantly shape public perception, understanding the underlying power structures becomes paramount. This study delves into the intricate dynamics of power as represented and perpetuated through media. This research employs a meticulous discourse analysis methodology, scrutinizing various forms of media content to unravel the strategies used to construct and depict dominance. By systematically deconstructing media narratives, this study uncovers the subtle, yet potent, mechanisms through which power is manifested and maintained. The findings reveal a complex interplay of language, imagery, and context, working in concert to normalize certain power hierarchies while marginalizing others. These representations are pivotal in shaping societal norms and individual worldviews, making the dissection of these narratives a task of critical importance. The implications of this research are manifold, offering profound insights for policymakers, media practitioners, and the public at large. By illuminating the covert structures of power embedded within media narratives, this study not only contributes to the academic discourse on media influence but also empowers stakeholders to foster more equitable and conscious media landscapes. As the media continues to evolve, understanding these power dynamics becomes essential in navigating and shaping the societal discourse.

The Historical Evolution of Interview Power Dynamics: A Comparative Analysis of Literary and Political Interviews

Indian Journal of Natural Sciences
Abstract: This study embarks on a comprehensive journey through the evolving landscape of interview power dynamics. It aims to dissect and map the progression of power relations in interviews, with a particular focus on the contrasts between literary and political interviews across various historical periods. Through meticulous examination, the paper illuminates the nuances and shifts in these power dynamics, drawing attention to both the parallels and divergences between literary and political contexts. Central to this analysis is an exploration of how the distinct objectives and formats of literary and political interviews influence their inherent power structures. While literary interviews typically center on promoting an author’s work and exploring the creative process, political interviews often strive to expose truth and hold figures of authority accountable. This contrast offers a unique vantage point for understanding the interplay of power in these two communicative settings. The study further delves into the subtleties of linguistic strategies, non-verbal cues, and the broader social contexts that frame these interviews. It examines how these elements collectively contribute to the construction, negotiation, and perception of power within interview interactions. A historical perspective provides a lens through which to observe changes in societal norms, values, and media practices, all of which are reflected in the shifting power dynamics of interviews over time. By comparing literary and political interviews, this paper offers both a historical account and a critical analysis of the factors that shape the flow of power in these interactions. The research uncovers the underlying forces—social, cultural, and media-driven—that define interview dynamics, thereby contributing valuable insights to the fields of media studies, communication, and social history. Ultimately, it advances a deeper understanding of power within one of the most fundamental forms of public discourse.

Voices of Change: The Role of Feminist Activism in Shaping Power Dynamics and Advancing Gender Equality

MFW 2024 Conference
Abstract: This paper delves into the transformative role of feminist activism in redefining societal power structures and advocating for gender equality. It adopts an interdisciplinary approach, integrating discourse analysis with empirical research to assess the impact of feminist activism on public discourse and policy. Focusing on the narratives, campaigns, and public engagements of feminist activists, the study draws from a diverse range of sources, including speeches, social media, and published works. It aims to understand how these activists utilize language and various forms of media to challenge traditional gender norms, dismantle patriarchal power structures, and foster a more equitable society. The analysis centers on both the content and form of these communications, exploring how feminist activists articulate their messages, mobilize support, and engage with opposition. In doing so, the paper highlights the successes of feminist movements in influencing public opinion and policy while also critically examining the challenges they face—such as cultural resistance, political barriers, and internal tensions within the movement. Through this comprehensive analysis, the paper offers a nuanced understanding of the complex interplay between feminist activism, power dynamics, and the pursuit of gender equality. This research contributes to the broader discourse on gender and power, providing valuable insights for academics, policymakers, activists, and all those interested in the role of activism in shaping societal change. The findings are expected to stimulate further research and dialogue on the evolving influence of feminist activism in transforming the landscape of gender equality.
